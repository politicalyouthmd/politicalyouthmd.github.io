ELECTED OFFICIALS

Annette Taddeo

State Senator District 40

Born: Barrancabermeja, Colombia

Current term ends: 11/08/2022

Party: Democrat

Bills Introduced:

Bill SB 1234: Provides any student as a resident to receive state financial aid not based on his or her immigration status.

Bill SB 1632: Revise the definition of “mortgage loan to remove a condition where the residential loans must be for personal, family, etc.

First Latina Democratic State Senator

Media Surrogate, Barack Obama Presidential Campaign, 2012

Supported Hillary Clinton in 2016’s election

Issue Positions:

Immigration: “All families, no matter their immigration status, are precious. No community should to live in fear of law enforcement and no child should prevented from attending Florida schools and providing a better life for their families.” She will continue to call on Congress to pass comprehensive immigration reform that protects DACA recipients.

Healthcare: Senator Taddeo believes in Medicare-for-All and expanding Medicaid in our state. With so many facing the impossible choice of paying for a medical bill or putting food on the table, Annette has proudly sponsored legislation that would put Medicaid expansion on the ballot and close the infamous coverage gap for over 800,000 residents of the Sunshine State.

Environment: Senator Taddeo has been a champion for a multi-faceted approach that keeps our water and our community safe--she has introduced legislation to ban fracking and has sought a ban on offshore drilling to protect our coastline.

Gun Violence: "In Congress, I won't bow to the gun lobby. I'll advocate for universal background checks, a ban on assault weapons, legislation that keeps guns out of the hands of domestic abusers, and to crack down on gun traffickers. It is time for us to end this epidemic.”

Education: In the Florida Senate, she introduced and co-sponsored bills that would study best practices for recruiting and retaining teachers, as well as ensures them a living wage so that they can best support our students and help them get ahead.

Minimum Wage: "By raising the minimum wage to $15 an hour by 2020, we could lift the wages of nearly one in four workers, as well as nearly 40 percent of working, single mothers. Parents working full-time shouldn't have to struggle to provide economic security for their family. We can instead ensure a living wage that will bring millions of hard-working Americans into the middle class.”


Joseph S. Geller

State House of Representatives District 100

Born: New York City, NY

Current term ends: 11/03/2020

Party: Democrat


Issue Positions:

Healthcare: “Abortion is a medical decision, not a legal one. It should be made by a woman and her doctor. I support Roe v. Wade.”

Education: “I support state standards and testing for K-12 students, but not in its current, high-stakes, one-size-fits-all form. I support the concept of No Child Left Behind, but it has been a failure due to, inter alia, lack of funding. Immigrant children should not be punished for their parents' actions. We need more money for education at all levels, and need to revise our tax structure in favor of a fairer, more equitable system to help find the money to fund education.”

Gun Violence: “Enforcement of current state law regarding guns is inadequate. The Assault Weapons Ban should be reenacted.”

Environment: “Alternative fuels and environmental protection are also areas of great potential job growth if we make smart investments now.”


Sharon Pritchett

State House of Representatives District 102

Born: Miami, FL

Current term ends: 11/03/2020

Party: Democrat


She worked with another state senator to sponsor legislation that would provide "mandatory vision screenings for students entering the public school system for the first time," explaining, "So often we hear about children who are just not performing to their optimal capabilities, and they're not doing so because they can't see; they've never had an eye exam. We need this. It's something that guarantees each child who may need corrective lenses can be identified at an early enough age."
