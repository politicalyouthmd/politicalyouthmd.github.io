POLICIES

Ordinances are county-wide legislation. They set local policy and can vary widely across an area. Below are some ordinances currently enacted in Miami-Dade.

The ordinance makes it unlawful for any person under the age of 17 to linger, stay, congregate, move about, wander or stroll in any public place in Miami-Dade County, either on foot or in a vehicle during curfew hours.

From Sunday night to Friday morning, the curfew lasts from 11 PM to 6 AM. From Friday evening to Sunday morning, the curfew lasts between midnight and 6 AM


    Exceptions:
  • Is accompanied by a legal guardian, or by someone 21 years of age or older with permission from the child's guardian
  • Is working or in transit to or from work
  • Is traveling interstate
  • Has written permission from a guardian
  • Is aiding or assisting in an emergency
  • Is traveling to or from a school, religious, civic or county-sponsored event
  • Is legally emancipated
  • Is homeless
  • Is exercising First Amendment rights
  • Is on their home's property, or a neighbor's property with permission
  • Is attending/returning from a public event which began before 10 p.m. and has written permission
  • Is authorized by the County Commission

    • If someone is found to be breaking the curfew, an officer will stop them, ask their age, why they are out, and then verify their answers with their guardian. If they don't have "cause" to be out, they will be taken home, to the police station, or "an appropriate holding facility".

    If someone is written up three or more times for breaking curfew, their guardian will receive a notice to appear and be punished with a fine of at most $500.

      The NAO addresses recurring criminal/nuisance activity involving:
  • The unlawful sale, delivery or possession of controlled substances/narcotics.
  • Youth and street gang criminal activity.
  • Prostitution or solicitation of prostitution.
  • Lewd and lascivious activity.
  • The illegal sale, possession, or consumption of alcoholic beverages.
  • Other criminal activities that are a danger to the public health, safety and welfare.

    • Private nuisances involving neighbor disputes are not addressed by the NAO. This includes:

  • Noise
  • Barking dogs
  • Loud music
  • Code enforcement
  • Other non-criminal activity

    • While these and other activities may be a community problem, they are not addressed by the NAO, but may be addressed through other Miami-Dade Police Department (MDPD) or Miami-Dade County entities. Call 311 to find out the best course of action.

    The Lauren Book Child Safety Ordinance made it against the law for a sexual predator to live within 2,500 feet of any school, park, or day care center.

    Though the county of Miami-Dade has said it does not take responsibility for the correctness of the information on the map of sexual offenders' residences found here it can be a useful tool for concerned parties.

    Living Wage: the level of pay that would ensure someone a "regular living standard" in a given area, typically calculated using expendatures such as medical expenses, transportation, food, shelter, etc. based on the number of people dependent on said salary.

    Currently, Miami's living wage ordinance covers contractors on city projects.
